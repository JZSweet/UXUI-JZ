Chores and who is (or isn't) doing them,
Can become a major source of conflict in the lives of many couples.

HoneyDew is here to help.

Overview

Couples often struggle to manage dividing household chores in a way that seems fair and equitable.


Honeydew wants to change that by making it fast and easy to divvy up housework.

By helping assign and track household tasks, couples be happier and have more time to spend on the things they enjoy.

Initial Research

User interviews

Through research we were seeking to answer these questions:

∎ What are our users looking for in an app like this?

∎ Would our users make a habit of using this app?

∎ Would using the app help users with time management?

∎ Do users feel the need to divide household tasks?

During our five interview, we discovered that cleaning, organizing, and lists were all very important, but our users need something that will hold their attention as well.

Competitor Research

OurHome is a chore app for families

+ Designed for couples, families, and shared living (roommates).

+ Offers rewards to users when chores completed.

− Interface is a little cluttered.

Spotless is an app to help users plan out weekly chores

+ Simple & clear organization features.

+ Abilities to set reminders.

− No sharing feature. One user only.

Habitica is gamified habit-forming app

+ The Gamified features are fun and engaging.

+ Allows user to sort tasks into multiple lists (To-do, Daily, Habits).

− Very feature heavy, could intimidate new users.

After analyzing our interview data and observing chore apps in the market,
we believe the market needs an app like HoneyDew specifically designed for people in a relationship.

Design Phase

Digital Wireframes

First, we started the design process with digital wireframes. That way, we could mock up something quickly and iterate on it with the first round of user testing.

With the wireframes, we were able to define the user flow and basic features of our app.



what users can do with the app:

∎ Users can create a profile, invite partners, and add chores to their homepage.

∎ Then they can choose to assign the chores to themself or their partner.

∎ After finishing the chores, they can mark as done and be awarded points for completion.

∎ The points can be spent on items in the avatar shop later on.

Usability Testing

After two user testing, we made the first iteration based on the feedback and changed some basic layouts.


The wireframe coaching screen used a toggle to assign chores between the user and their partner. We quickly learned that was confusing for the users. Therefore we changed it in the checkboxes.
