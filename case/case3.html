Overview

GoalGetter is a centralized digital place to help middle school students keep track of an ongoing list of assignments and class deadlines during the pandemic.

We believe that when given the correct tool, kids can easily practice the skill of organization.

Initial Research

Brainstorm

This project began because of a personal endeavor.
My friend Kristi told us over a chat about how challenging it is to manage her children's schoolwork during this pandemic, so we brainstormed an app idea to help with her situation.

Kristi has six kids, each with unique needs and personalities. Some of them she can put on autopilot because they are straight-A students who are incredibly self-affection. Others may have a hard time staying focused or struggle to keep track of their hard copy school material. The circumstances worsened after the pandemic forced students to transition from in-person to remote learning without warning or practice.

User Interviews

After seven interviews with both students and parents, my team and I found out that when homework is distributed digitally and by hard copy can cause students to turn in assignments late inadvertently, receive lower grades, and become discouraged at school.

Empathy Map

We built an empathy map to articulate what we knew about our target user base on the interviews data. It helped us create a shared understanding of user needs and aid in decision making.

Middle schoolers are a unique demographic. They straddled two worlds as they evolved from being young kids to young adults. Each individual makes this journey at a vastly different speed.

We believe there is a huge opportunity to transform the way middle schoolers manage their schoolwork into a fun and delightful experience. Our successes will be measurable by improved grades and motivation.

UI Inspiration & Style Tile

Parkour is a word Kristi's kids use when they talk about a shared passion. We thought the word Parkour - Athletic training to get from Point A to B in the most creative and efficient way possible, fit our core values. So we took inspertion from it and built out UI style tile and the logo.

Design Phase

Lo-Fidelity Prototype

First Usability Testing

We started the design process with digital wireframes. After first cycle of testing, we iteratied on our Lo-fidelity Wireframes. We prioritized three main changes:

∎Add a way for users to take notes.

∎Update terminology in the main navigation to help users understand.

∎Add the “Goals” page to the scope of our project.

Mid-Fidelity Prototype

Second Usability Testing

After second cycle of testing, we iteratied on our Mid-Fidelity Prototype. We prioritized five main changes:

∎Subjects Page - Add micro text to the class card icons so the purpose is clear.

∎Calendar Page - Use icons/color to distinguish what assignments have been turned in, and include an inspirational quote at the top of the page.

∎To-Do List Page - Minimize the colors to avoid visual confusion.

∎Goals Page - Add a way to input goals and rewards.
